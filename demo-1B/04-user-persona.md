# User Persona: Vibe Code Academy

## Primary Persona

### Name
Alex Morgan

### Persona Type
Smart beginner with a real product idea but limited coding confidence.

### Short Summary
Alex has ideas for websites, apps, automations, or startup concepts, but gets stuck when it is time to turn those ideas into something visible and testable. They have used ChatGPT before and understand that AI is powerful, but they do not yet have a reliable workflow for planning, prompting, building, testing, and improving a prototype.

---

## Background

Alex may be a student, marketer, early-stage founder, operator, freelancer, or career-switcher.

They are comfortable using digital tools, but they do not think of themselves as a software engineer. They may have experimented with ChatGPT, Claude, Replit, Lovable, or other AI tools, but their results have been inconsistent.

They are interested in AI because it seems like a way to move faster, build ideas, and become more capable without waiting for a technical cofounder or developer.

---

## Goals

Alex wants to:

- Turn rough product ideas into something visible.
- Learn how to use AI tools without feeling lost.
- Understand how to give better instructions to AI.
- Build a simple prototype they can share with others.
- Get more confident working inside a project folder.
- Learn enough about software structure to avoid being fooled by polished but shallow outputs.
- Leave with a repeatable process they can use again.

---

## Pain Points

Alex struggles with:

- Not knowing where to start.
- Prompting AI too vaguely.
- Getting outputs that look polished but feel generic.
- Not knowing whether the AI-generated work is actually correct.
- Confusing a mockup with a real product.
- Losing context across long AI chats.
- Feeling intimidated by code editors, files, and technical vocabulary.
- Not knowing how to break a project into manageable steps.

---

## Current Behavior

Alex often starts with a vague prompt like:

> Build me an app for my idea.

The AI produces something that looks impressive at first, but then Alex gets stuck when they need to revise it, test it, connect it to real functionality, or explain what the prototype actually proves.

Alex may also bounce between tools, trying ChatGPT, Lovable, Replit, or Cursor without understanding which tool is best for which stage of the process.

---

## Motivations

Alex is motivated by:

- Seeing an idea become real.
- Getting unstuck.
- Building confidence with AI.
- Creating something they can show a teacher, teammate, client, investor, or employer.
- Learning practical skills that feel immediately useful.
- Understanding the difference between a pretty demo and a useful prototype.

---

## Fears and Objections

Alex may worry:

- “I am not technical enough.”
- “Cursor will be too advanced for me.”
- “AI might write code I do not understand.”
- “I will break something.”
- “My idea is too vague.”
- “Everyone else already knows more than I do.”
- “I do not know what counts as a good prototype.”

Vibe Code Academy should reduce these fears by making the process feel structured, practical, and beginner-friendly.

---

## What Alex Needs From Vibe Code Academy

Alex needs:

1. A simple mental model for AI-assisted building.
2. A clear distinction between planning and building.
3. A guided workflow they can follow step by step.
4. Examples of weak prompts versus better prompts.
5. A safe introduction to Cursor and project folders.
6. A simple prototype assignment with realistic scope.
7. Feedback on what is clear, confusing, or missing.
8. A way to document progress and next steps.

---

## Key Message For This Persona

You do not need to become a software engineer overnight.

You do need to learn how to think clearly, plan the work, guide the AI, test the output, and improve the result.

Vibe Code Academy helps you move from idea to prototype without pretending AI is magic.

---

## Desired Transformation

### Before
Alex has an idea, but it exists mostly in their head, notes, or a chat conversation.

### After
Alex has a simple prototype artifact, a clearer product explanation, feedback from another person, and a practical next-step plan.

---

## Success Criteria

Vibe Code Academy succeeds for Alex if they leave able to:

- Explain their product idea clearly.
- Identify the target user and problem.
- Write a simple project brief.
- Use Cursor to create or revise a prototype.
- Understand what is real versus simulated in the prototype.
- Test the prototype with another person.
- Decide what to improve next.

## Secondary Personas

### The Marketing Operator
A marketer or business operator who wants to use AI to create landing pages, campaign tools, reports, internal dashboards, or workflow prototypes.

### The Early-Stage Founder
A founder with a product idea who wants to make the concept visible before hiring developers or spending money on a full build.

### The Student Builder
A student who needs to present a product concept, final project, report, or pitch and wants a prototype to make the idea easier to understand.

### The Curious Professional
A working professional who knows AI is becoming important and wants a practical way to start building, not just reading about tools.

---

## Tone Implications

For this persona, the brand voice should be:

- Clear, not clever for its own sake.
- Practical, not theoretical.
- Confident, not hype-driven.
- Encouraging, but honest about limitations.
- Beginner-friendly, but not childish.
- Focused on artifacts, not abstract AI excitement.

---

## Product Implications

The landing page should emphasize:

- A concrete outcome: build a simple prototype.
- A low-pressure starting point: no advanced coding required.
- A practical workflow: plan, build, test, improve, share.
- A clear tool focus: Cursor.
- A useful lead magnet: the Prototype Starter Kit.
- Reassurance: AI helps you move faster, but you remain responsible for thinking and testing.

---
