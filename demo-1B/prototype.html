The gap

You have the idea. AI does not know what to build yet.

Most people get stuck between talking about an idea and making something real. AI tools are powerful, but without structure you end up with vague prompts, generic output, and no way to judge what is useful.

Stuck

Prompts too vague

“Build me an app for my idea” produces something that looks impressive until you need to revise, test, or explain what it proves.

Risk

Polish hides weak thinking

A slick UI can mask fake functionality, unclear users, and missing product logic. Pretty is not the same as useful.

Fix

Process beats magic

AI is an execution layer. It moves fast when you provide direction, context, constraints, and feedback.

What you leave with

A prototype artifact—not a finished product.

By the end of the workshop you can explain your idea clearly, show a simple build, understand what is real versus simulated, and decide what to improve next.

Explain the product

Build inside Cursor

Test with a real person

Repeat the workflow

How it works

Plan → Build → Test → Improve → Share

One focused workshop walks you through the full loop. You are not learning to become a software engineer overnight—you are learning to direct AI-assisted work with Cursor in a real project folder.

Plan

Define user, problem, scope, and what the prototype needs to prove before you ask AI to build.

Build

Guide Cursor phase by phase—not one endless chat thread.

Test

Run the prototype with another person. Capture what works and what confuses.

Improve

Revise based on feedback. Know what is simulated vs functional in your demo.

Share

Document progress and next steps so the artifact is ready to show a teammate, client, or class.

FAQ

Straight answers for beginners.

Do I need to know how to code?

No advanced coding required. You will work inside Cursor with guided steps. Clear thinking matters more than syntax memorization.

What tools will we use?

Primarily Cursor, plus planning templates and project files. You learn how to keep context in a folder instead of losing it in chat.

Is this a finished software product or a prototype?

A prototype artifact you can show and test—not production-ready software. Engineering, payments, and deployment are out of workshop scope.

What happens after I sign up for the starter kit?

You receive the kit materials (simulated in this demo). From there you can join an intro ($149) or full workshop ($299) when you want guided help.

Is this workshop a fit if I am not technical?

Yes, if you have a real idea and are willing to plan before you prompt. The class is built for smart beginners, not career engineers.

Does the signup form send real email in this prototype?

No. This page is a teaching demo. Signup is simulated in a later build phase—nothing is stored or emailed until you wire a real backend.

Will AI build the whole thing for me?

AI can build fast—it still needs your direction. We teach you to plan, prompt, review, and test so you stay responsible for the result.
